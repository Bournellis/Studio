## Purpose

This document defines the first narrative direction for RPG Turnos.

The current runtime is still a playable mechanical slice. Names currently present in JSON, scenes, assets, and UI are placeholders unless this document promotes them.

## Core Premise

RPG Turnos initially follows a Draxos invasion of an elemental planet.

The Draxos are arcane intergalactic beings who use astral energy for nearly everything: combat, travel, control, construction, and domination. Their culture treats many other beings as inferior and suitable for enslavement.

The invasion has three central objectives:

- dominate a planet with an enormous concentration of astral energy
- enslave elemental beings who inhabit the planet
- extract a huge and powerful crystal inside a volcano

The campaign is not about defending the planet. The player starts on the invading side.

## Player Character

The player character is a novice Draxos mage.

Initial truths:

- recently completed training
- assigned to a respected Draxos strike team
- joins because the team lost one soldier in its previous battle
- starts with low status inside the team
- grows through practical missions, battlefield success, and problem solving

The novice's personal name, personality, and exact class title are still TBD.

## Draxos Strike Team

The player belongs to an elite and respected Draxos team.

The team has a reputation for competence, but its recent record is damaged in secret. In previous operations, other beings surprised them, stopped them, or stole what they were trying to acquire.

Only the team currently knows how serious those failures were.

Their immediate motivation is to return home with a major victory before their recent failures become public inside Draxos society.

## Ether-Plasm Base

The starting hub is a Draxos base that resembles a spacecraft from a human point of view.

It is not technological. It is plasmated from ether and sustained by Draxos astral craft.

Expected hub functions:

- starting point for the novice
- command center for the invasion
- place to meet the commander and specialist mages
- mission selection and briefing surface
- character progression, class growth, and deck evolution surface

## Campaign Arc

The campaign starts with small tasks and grows toward strategic responsibility.

### Early Missions

The novice receives limited assignments on the elemental planet:

- fight small elemental groups
- secure strategic areas
- test readiness in real operations
- prove usefulness to the commander and senior mages

### Mid Campaign

Other members of the team begin having difficulties.

The novice is asked to solve problems that should have belonged to stronger members of the team. This raises the player's status and creates the gameplay reason for more abilities, cards, and deck options.

### Late Campaign

The mission pushes toward the volcano and the powerful crystal inside it.

The Draxos must overcome stronger elemental resistance and complications caused by earlier hidden interference.

Near the final test, another race appears openly to help the elementals. This race was already helping secretly before the reveal. Its identity is intentionally TBD.

## Gameplay Progression Meaning

Mechanical growth should map to narrative status.

As the novice gains status inside the Draxos team, the player gains:

- better class tools
- more abilities
- more cards
- broader deck options
- stronger mission access

The first implementation should focus on one Draxos mage class. Later classes may have different character growth, starter decks, upgrades, and play styles.

## Initial Class Direction

Only one player class should be designed first.

Open decisions for the first class:

- class name
- astral specialty
- starting deck identity
- upgrade path
- relationship between hero power and card pool
- whether cards represent spells, bound elementals, astral commands, constructs, or mission assets

Do not design multiple classes until the first class has a strong playable and narrative baseline.
