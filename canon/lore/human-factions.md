# Human Factions

- Last Updated: `2026-05-14`
- Status: `active shared lore canon`

## Purpose

This document defines the concept and nature of human factions in the shared universe.

Individual factions — their names, territories, specific cultures, and story roles — are authored per project as needed. This document governs what factions are, how they emerged, and how they relate to each other.

---

## What Factions Are

Human factions are political-social forms of coexistence. Each one represents a distinct type of society with its own structure of power, hierarchy, and rules.

Factions are not defined by their technology. A faction's identity comes from how its people live together — who holds power, how decisions are made, what is permitted, what is punished, what is valued, and how that society treats those inside and outside it.

Examples of the range that may exist: rigid military hierarchies, decentralized horizontal networks, theocracies, corporate states, expanded family clans, and everything in between. The specific shape of a faction is a product of its history, not a design choice imposed from outside.

---

## How Factions Emerged

The fracturing began during the colonization of the Solar System, when humanity was already divided into competing groups. The nuclear apocalypse on Earth accelerated everything.

Without the planet as a common center, the groups that survived in space lost the gravitational pull that had kept human civilization loosely unified. Each faction was left to solve the problem of survival in space on its own terms, with its own resources, neighbors, and threats.

Over millennia, those different survival strategies hardened into culture. The pressures of a specific environment — resource scarcity, proximity to particular alien races, access to particular forms of energy or trade — shaped what each society became. What started as tactical adaptations became identity.

The result is that human factions today are not variations on the same template. They are distinct peoples who share a biological origin and a catastrophic history, but who have evolved along radically different paths.

---

## The Compatibility Question

The divergence over millennia has reached a point where many seriously question whether the different human factions are still compatible — not just politically, but at a deeper level of values, biology, and worldview.

Some factions have modified themselves through technology, astral practice, or prolonged contact with non-human races to a degree that makes them functionally distinct from other humans. Others have remained more conservative in their development.

This is not a resolved question in the lore. It is an active tension that different projects may explore in different ways.

---

## Inter-Faction Dynamics

The relationship between human factions is not static. It is an active web of alliances, neutrality pacts, trade agreements, cold conflicts, and open wars that shifts based on interest, threat, and opportunity.

Key dynamics:

- **Alliances** form around shared threats, complementary resources, or cultural affinity. They are maintained by mutual benefit, not by sentiment.
- **Neutrality** is often a strategic posture rather than genuine indifference. A neutral faction may be waiting, positioning, or simply lacking the power to commit to a side.
- **Wars** between factions are not rare. The absence of a central authority means disputes are resolved by power, negotiation, or attrition — not by an overarching law.

The presence of non-human beings adds another layer to this dynamic. Some factions have close alliances with specific alien races. Others are hostile to all non-human contact. Most are somewhere between.

---

## Factions and the Astral Dimension

The astral dimension of reality is not equally accessible or equally embraced by all factions.

Some factions have developed deep astral practices — spiritual, martial, or infrastructural — as a central part of their identity. Others have rejected the astral in favor of purely technological approaches. Most occupy a middle ground, using astral elements where useful without making them central to their identity.

This is one of the major axes of differentiation between factions and one of the sources of genuine incompatibility between some groups.

---

## Rule for Individual Factions

Individual factions — with names, territories, cultures, and story roles — are authored in project-local documents when needed.

A faction becomes canonical when it is explicitly documented with a name, a defined social structure, a place in the inter-faction web, and at least one project using it as active story material.

Until then, it is a design placeholder.
